# Brand, trademark & trade dress

The DeMoD UI framework is free and open under the Mozilla Public License 2.0. The
**code** is open; the **brand** is not. The MPL grants rights in the source code only —
it grants **no** rights to use the licensor's trademarks or trade dress (MPL-2.0 §2.3).

This is the engineering statement of DeMoD LLC's brand position. It is **not legal
advice** and is pending review by counsel.

## Trademarks (common-law ™)

**"DeMoD" and "TERMINUS"** are claimed as trademarks of **DeMoD LLC**, asserted through
use (™). We make no claim of federal registration here; registration (®) may be pursued
later. Because the names are relatively common words, the marks are modest — they deter
casual copying of the *name*, and are not the primary protection.

## Trade dress (the emphasis)

The asset DeMoD principally relies on is **trade dress** — the distinctive **total visual
impression** of DeMoD/TERMINUS products, taken as a **whole**. No single element below is
claimed in isolation (a Sierpinski triangle and the word "DeMoD" are common); the
protected thing is the **combination**:

- the oscilloscope-phosphor color identity — turquoise `#00F5D4` + violet `#8B5CF6` on
  deep black `#0A0A0F`,
- the CRT treatment — scanlines + edge vignette + a slow background pulse,
- the Sierpinski-glow motif used as the identity mark and boot/assemble animation,
- the fixed 8×16 monospace "phosphor" type,
- the blade / coverflow shell layout and its focus-field navigation feel,

assembled into one recognizable look. That assembled look is the product's source
identifier.

### Important boundaries (what trade dress does and doesn't reach)

- **Functional features are not trade dress.** Software rendering, the drawing
  primitives, the palette *as data/constants*, and the Sierpinski primitive are
  **functional capability** shipped under the MPL — anyone may use them. Trade dress
  covers the *non-functional, source-identifying* overall appearance, not these building
  blocks.
- **It is a claim to develop, not a present registration.** Trade dress in product design
  requires *secondary meaning* (the public coming to associate the look with DeMoD),
  earned through use over time. This document states the claim; it does not by itself
  establish it.
- **The MPL covers code, not brand.** The license lets you fork and build freely; it does
  not grant the DeMoD brand or trade dress.

## What you can do

- Fork the framework, modify it, and build your own apps, instruments, and products on it
  — commercial or not, open or proprietary.
- Use the generic building blocks (primitives, color constants, the Sierpinski helper) to
  create **your own distinct look**. This is encouraged.
- State, factually, that your product is "built on" or "uses the DeMoD UI framework."
- Keep the existing copyright and license notices in the source (the MPL requires this).

## What you may not do

- Use "DeMoD" or "TERMINUS" (or confusingly similar names) to name or brand your own
  product, service, distribution, or fork.
- **Reproduce DeMoD's assembled look-and-feel** (the combination above) as the identity of
  your product, or otherwise in a way that suggests it is DeMoD's, or that DeMoD endorses,
  sponsors, or is affiliated with it.
- Use the DeMoD identity/marks to brand a competing product.
